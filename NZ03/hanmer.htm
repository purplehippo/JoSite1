HANMER SPRINGS day

James (seen below at Lake Tekapo) bravely took me for a ride in his car to Hanmer Springs (bravely because I had to drive back!).

Stopping at Kaiapoi, this is the oldest working steamer in NZ. The Tuhoe.
Built in 1919 in Auckland. It became a fishing vessel in 1963, but declared unseaworthy, laid up at Kaiapoi and converted into a floating art gallery and hand-craft exhibition centre.
In 1981, the vessel was restored. At present it is owned by the M.V.Tuhoe Preservation Society. The Tuhoe runs cruises on the Kaiapoi and Waimakariri Rivers. Obviously, an 'A Class' no 428. Mmmmm. There was a very chatty man here. Told us loads and loads and loads and .... of stuff. Very helpful if we had all day.
From their website, Weka Pass Railway, this A class 428 was built for New Zealand Rail in Thames, New Zealand in 1909. It ran on the main trunk line, mainly on express trains. The engine was saved from the scrap heap in the 1960's. This is one of only two in existence (and the only one in working steam condition).
