ORANA WILDLIFE PARK

Anna, Shar (housie mates) and I went to Orana Wildlife Park, McLeans Island, Christchurch on Easter Monday, 21 April 2003. Orana is an open range wildlife Park with the design reflecting the animals' natural habitats. Orana is a Maori word for welcome or place of refuge.

Unfortunately, due to slow modems, I've only put a few pix up. There are fab photos of Turtles, Zebras, Emus, Bulls, Camels, Reptiles, Springboks, Ostriches, Giraffes, Ring Tailed Lemurs and Black and White Lemurs missing :-(

We paid our pennies, well, gave away all our cents, and went for a wonder. Meer Cats. OK, so animals is animals - you should know what they are. But they're all cute. Just like...?? Kanga is a Red Kangaroo. Even though he's grey. The lion mum being protective of her 'ikkle cubs. Arrrrr. Mmmmm. Yummy mud. After a quick sock chase (put on for the spectators), time for a quick prowl around. Hey, hey, we're the monkeys.... Long Tail Monkeys to be precise. Yuk. What's this? Don't know what these, rare species have been fed. Not sure I want any. Perhaps it's a hang-over?? Yep, this looks terra-torial (hahahaaa) and visious. I lost an eyebrow since I had this taken!! :-)
